Things I Do

jobs and things

I work about eight hours each week at Mathnasium, helping younger students gain confidence in math through tutoring and guiding them with problems. I also babysit my younger brother and his friends— which definitely requires creativity and patience.

Volunteering

  • Museum of Science – Gallery Learning Volunteer

    Every Sunday morning, I volunteer at Boston’s Museum of Science, assisting with hands-on exhibits and programs. I’ve supported activities in the Engineering Design Workshop and the Hall of Human Life, helping visitors explore and learn through interactive tasks.

  • Library of Congress – Remote Volunteer

    I volunteer remotely for the Library of Congress, transcribing historical documents to help preserve and digitize important archives for public access.

  • Smithsonian Institution – Remote Volunteer

    Similarly, I contribute to Smithsonian projects, transcribing field notes, letters, and journals online.

  • Key Club – 100+ Hours of Service

    As a dedicated Key Club member, I’ve completed over 100 hours volunteering in my community. My work has included food drives, book donations, neighborhood clean-ups, and mentoring younger students through various local initiatives.

at school

DECA, Key Club, Ceramics, Model UN, Quiz Bowl

At school, I’m involved in DECA, Key Club, Ceramics, Model UN, and Quiz Bowl. I compete in DECA and went to states last year. I enjoy ceramics, creating bowls and dishes, attend Model UN conferences, and help my school’s Quiz Bowl team. This year, I’ve also joined Math Team, Girls Who Code, and Theater Tech.

summer stuff

  • 2025 - EDW at MIT, Harvard Academies

    • EDW stands for Engineering Design Workshop. It’s a summer program at MIT where I get to work on real engineering projects with other students. We spend time brainstorming, building, and testing things, which is super hands-on and really fun. At Harvard Academies, I took part in a one-week program all about financial consulting. It was intense but gave me a peek into how businesses solve money-related problems.

  • 2024 - Artemis at BU, 360 Squash, Fessenden Squash

    • Artemis is a computer science summer program at Boston University just for girls. We learned lots about coding and computer science ideas, and worked on cool projects together. I also played squash in two different programs — 360 Squash and Fessenden Squash. Both helped me get better at the game and taught me about teamwork and discipline.

  • 2023 - Weston Drama Workshop

    • In 2023, I joined the Weston Drama Workshop and took part in their production of Grease. It was my first real experience in theater, where I learned about acting, stage production, and how everyone works together to put on a show. It was a lot of fun and a great way to try something new.

  • 2022 - ECO Camp Wellesley, Linx

    • ECO Camp Wellesley is an environmental camp where I learned about nature, ecology, and ways to help the planet. It was outdoors a lot and really opened my eyes to conservation. At Linx, I was part of their middle-school theater production of Descendants. It was a lively show, and I enjoyed being part of a creative team.
